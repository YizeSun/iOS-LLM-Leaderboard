# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

Contributors, reviewers, and maintainers are expected to communicate
respectfully, discuss evidence rather than people, disclose relevant
affiliations, and help keep benchmark participation safe and reviewable.

Acceptable disagreement includes challenging a protocol, result, review, or
interpretation with reproducible evidence and clear reasoning.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, discrimination, threats, sexualized
conduct, doxxing, publishing private device or account information, deliberate
benchmark fabrication, concealing failed attempts, pressuring contributors to
selectively rerun results, impersonation, or retaliation for good-faith review.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
benchmark submissions, project-controlled community spaces, and public
representation of the project.

## Enforcement

Report ordinary concerns to the maintainers listed in
[`MAINTAINERS.md`](MAINTAINERS.md). Do not place personal data, credentials, or
sensitive evidence in a public issue. For a sensitive report, first ask a
maintainer through GitHub for a private channel and provide only the minimum
non-sensitive context publicly.

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions, pause review, exclude evidence,
issue a warning, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban a
participant. Enforcement decisions should identify the violated rule and avoid
unnecessary disclosure of the reporter or affected person.

Good-faith reports and benchmark failures must not be used as grounds for
retaliation.
